Ferrari


The new V6 Ferraris, specifically the 296 GTB and the limited-edition F80 hypercar, mark a new era for the brand, integrating highly advanced hybrid technology and a powerful 3.0-litre twin-turbo V6 engine derived from their Le Mans-winning 499P race car. The 296 GTB, available as a coupe or GTS spider with a retractable hardtop, delivers an impressive 819 horsepower (830 CV) and can operate in a silent, electric-only "e-Drive" mode for short distances. The even more potent F80, the long-awaited successor to the LaFerrari, pushes the boundaries further with a combined 1,183 horsepower from its hybrid system, making it the most powerful road-legal Ferrari ever produced and promising electrifying performance on both road and track. Despite initial skepticism from traditionalists, engineers chose the V6 for its compact size, light weight, and power-dense architecture, which allows for superior aerodynamics and handling characteristics, proving that forced induction and electrification can enhance, not diminish, the legendary Ferrari driving experience.
